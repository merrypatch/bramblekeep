## Which one applies to me?

### The AGPL is enough for you if…

- You **self-host Bramblekeep for yourself or your organization's internal use** — no obligation triggered, even if you modify it, as long as you don't offer the modified version to third parties.
- You **run Bramblekeep unmodified** and simply make it available to others — keep the notices and point users to this source repository.
- You **fork and improve Bramblekeep and publish your changes under the AGPL** — exactly what open source is for. Welcome.

> **The AGPL network clause (§13).** Because Bramblekeep is a web application, making a **modified** version available to users **over a network** counts as distribution: you must offer those users the complete corresponding source of your modified version, under the AGPL. There is no "private SaaS" loophole.

### You need the commercial license if…

- You want to **offer a modified Bramblekeep as a hosted/SaaS product** to third parties **without publishing your modifications** under the AGPL.
- You want to **embed Bramblekeep in a proprietary/closed-source product** and distribute it.
- Your organization's **legal policy forbids AGPL** software, even for internal use.

In short: the commercial license buys you an **exemption from the AGPL's copyleft obligations**. Everything else stays the same software.
